# Studio Charter

## Mission

Turn creative and technical ambiguity into compact, reviewable, implementation-ready visual packages for small production teams.

## Customer promise

The buyer receives more than visual files. Every delivery includes:

- Finished production assets
- Editable source files
- Target-platform exports
- Naming and folder consistency
- Integration notes or a working engine package
- QA evidence
- Asset provenance and manifest data
- A concise handoff guide

## What the studio is

- A specialist production unit
- A rapid prototyping partner
- A white-label overflow team
- A bridge between visual creation and technical delivery
- A research-to-deliverable operator

## What the studio is not

- An unlimited design subscription
- A generic prompt-to-image vendor
- A full game-development company
- A branding agency responsible for discovering an entire visual identity in five days
- A replacement for client creative direction
- A large-scale VFX house
- A long-term software consultancy unless separately scoped

## Buyer prerequisites

The best client has:

- A real deadline or production milestone
- An approved or nearly approved visual direction
- A named reviewer
- Source materials they have the right to use
- A bounded asset list or prototype objective
- A target engine, platform, format, or review environment
- Budget authority for a paid pilot

## Default exclusions

Unless explicitly included in scope:

- Voice acting
- Music and sound design
- Full game logic
- Backend systems
- Extensive 3D production
- Character rigging for complex skeletal pipelines
- Unlimited revisions
- Original brand strategy
- Legal clearance of client-supplied materials
- Physical installation or hardware deployment

## Production principles

1. **Complete frames over fragments.** Final animation frames include the base artwork and effects required for coherent playback.
2. **Approved style before volume.** No batch production begins before a representative style proof is approved.
3. **Technical delivery is part of the product.** Engine imports, pivots, dimensions, formats, and naming are validated before handoff.
4. **Every asset is traceable.** Supplied, original, modified, and AI-assisted materials are identified.
5. **One-week does not mean unbounded.** Speed is achieved through controlled scope, not hidden overtime.
6. **Artifacts must survive handoff.** A new team member should understand the package without a meeting.
7. **AI assists production; it does not replace review.** Generated or transformed assets pass the same visual, technical, and rights gates as manual work.

## Success metrics

- At least 90% of package files accepted without correction
- Zero missing source or runtime files
- Zero critical engine-import failures
- One consolidated revision round or fewer
- Delivery within five production days after scope lock
- Manifest completeness above 95%
- Buyer able to open, review, and extend the package without studio intervention
