# Intake and Qualification

## Qualification questions

1. What must exist at the end of five production days?
2. Who approves the work?
3. What visual direction is already approved?
4. What source files and rights are available?
5. What engine, platform, dimensions, formats, and versions are required?
6. What is the maximum asset count and complexity?
7. What is the real milestone or publishing date?
8. What would make the delivery unacceptable?
9. What budget can be authorized for the pilot?
10. Is AI-assisted production allowed, restricted, or disclosure-dependent?

## Accept the project when

- The buyer can name the deliverables
- The buyer has one decision-maker
- Inputs can arrive before Day 1
- Rights and confidentiality are understood
- Scope fits a standard package
- The target platform is supported
- Acceptance tests can be agreed

## Reject or rescope when

- The buyer wants unlimited concepts or revisions
- No one can approve the style
- Required source materials are missing
- The deadline predates the input handoff
- The client expects full software development inside an art package
- The client cannot state a budget range
- The project requires unsupported 3D, film, or enterprise-security capabilities
- Rights to source material are unclear
