# Vertical Scout — Short-Form Motion Advertising

## Selected vertical

Small motion-design, VFX, and advertising studios that turn approved still artwork into short looping promotional assets.

The interested slice is not the entire advertising market. It is studios with:

- fast-turnaround social, storefront, Kickstarter, or product-launch work;
- existing client artwork that needs motion rather than a new campaign identity;
- recurring demand for 5–15 second loops and platform-specific exports;
- limited capacity for pixel cleanup, compositing, or effects variations;
- a preference for white-label or overflow production.

## Public demand signals

- [LoopCraft Studio](https://www.loopcraftstudio.com/) publicly positions around 15-second motion ads, 48-hour concept-to-delivery, e-commerce, Kickstarter, and local-business campaigns.
- [Nala Studio](https://naladesignstudio.com/services) offers illustration-led motion, VFX-style motion elements, and short looping GIFs for social, email, and websites.
- [Pixel Ninja](https://www.pixelninja.design/about) describes itself as an independent motion-design and VFX studio serving businesses and post-production partners.
- [Pigeon Studio](https://studiopigeon.com/portfolio/) lists commercial animation, motion design, character design, and social campaigns across B2B, B2C, startup, and software clients.
- [Whoosh Studios](https://www.whooshstudios.com/) serves broadcasters, agencies, production companies, and brands with 2D/3D motion graphics for digital and social channels.

These signals support a focused production-operations offer, not a claim that every studio needs outsourced AI work.

## Proposed offer

### Integrated Motion Loop Sprint

Input:

- one approved still, illustration, product image, mascot, or campaign frame;
- target platform and aspect ratios;
- approved style and rights confirmation;
- one short motion brief.

Output:

- one 5–15 second integrated motion loop;
- complete animated frames or a rendered video, depending on delivery;
- 9:16, 1:1, and 16:9 exports when required;
- source layers and reusable effect assets where appropriate;
- one consolidated revision;
- provenance manifest and QA report.

The production output must be a temporally coherent complete animation. A static image with a detached animated overlay is a prototyping technique only, not the final standard.

## One-week pilot

- Day 0: source audit, rights check, motion brief, acceptance criteria
- Day 1: motion map and first style proof
- Day 2: complete frame or shot production
- Day 3: integrated VFX and timing pass
- Day 4: cleanup, platform variants, client revision
- Day 5: QA, renders, manifest, handoff

Suggested pilot scope:

- one source image;
- one 5–8 second loop;
- six to twelve integrated frames or a short rendered clip;
- maximum two effect families;
- three export formats;
- one revision.

Estimated pilot range: **$500–$1,500**, subject to source quality, resolution, and revision limits.

## Why our capabilities fit

- Original raster and pixel-art production
- Complete frame-by-frame animation
- Integrated 2D VFX and compositing
- Static-to-motion treatment with reusable authoring layers
- Rapid one-week production control
- Asset manifests, provenance, and technical handoff
- Repeatable model evaluation without making the client manage models

## Acceptance tests

- source artwork is preserved and recognizable;
- motion reads as one integrated shot;
- no detached or floating effect layer;
- no frame drift, flicker, broken arcs, or accidental repainting;
- output passes target dimensions, alpha, palette, and frame-rate checks;
- exports are ready for the requested platform;
- all source and runtime files are accounted for in the manifest.

## First outreach angle

Offer a **single integrated motion proof from one existing client asset**. Lead with faster capacity for short loops, platform variants, and effects cleanup. Do not lead with “AI”; lead with the bounded production result and the reviewable handoff.

## Evidence still needed

Before commercial outreach, gather:

1. one publicly visible studio case where a still asset was turned into motion;
2. the studio’s preferred delivery formats and frame rates;
3. evidence of current overflow, turnaround, or production-capacity pressure;
4. a named creative producer, motion lead, or post-production contact;
5. a customer-provided sample asset before any production proposal.

## Readiness

**Conditional.** The vertical is commercially plausible and has visible demand signals, but it needs one internal benchmark and one validated prospect conversation before promotion to an active sales package.
