# Launch Roadmap

## Stage 0 — Initialized

Completed in this pack:

- Studio position and boundaries
- Capability registry
- Service catalog
- Standard one-week production cycle
- Package definitions
- Intake and qualification workflow
- QA gates
- Delivery manifest template
- Pricing and proposal framework

## Stage 1 — Internal proof

Complete one benchmark for each of the first three packages:

1. Godot Vertical-Slice Art Pack
2. LiveOps Asset Drop
3. Mascot Motion Expansion Pack

For every benchmark, record:

- Total human hours
- Production and compute cost
- Number of revisions
- Defects found at each gate
- Percentage of files accepted without rework
- Packaging time
- Import and playback results

### Promotion gate

A package becomes externally sellable only if:

- It completes within five production days
- It stays inside target labor cost
- It passes all critical QA gates
- A reviewer can use the package without verbal support
- The final manifest is complete

## Stage 2 — Paid pilots

Target three paid pilots:

- One small game or LiveOps studio
- One mascot, motion, or performance-creative buyer
- One learning, exhibit, or interactive agency

Do not discount below cost. Reduce scope instead of lowering standards.

## Stage 3 — Repeatability

After three pilots:

- Compare estimated versus actual hours
- Remove deliverables that cause unpredictable rework
- Convert repeat requests into priced add-ons
- Tighten complexity tiers
- Create before-and-after case studies
- Establish a standard rush premium

## Stage 4 — Channel partnerships

Approach:

- Motion and brand studios
- Exhibit agencies
- Learning-product vendors
- Game co-development firms

Offer a white-label production sprint only after the studio has proven response time, source hygiene, confidentiality, and delivery consistency.

## Stage 5 — Model operations

Launch the Creative Model Evaluation Pack only after:

- A reproducible evaluation harness exists
- Licensing and data-use review are documented
- Cost and latency reporting is reliable
- Deployment and rollback are tested
- One public or anonymized benchmark can be shown
